## Introduction

Student dropout is a major issue in higher education institutions worldwide. Many students fail to complete their degrees due to academic, financial, or personal challenges. This not only impacts the students’ future but also leads to significant losses for universities in terms of time, resources, and reputation.

Traditional methods used by institutions to identify at-risk students are often reactive, meaning intervention happens only after the student is already struggling. This highlights the need for proactive approaches that can detect early warning signs of dropout.

In this project, we apply data mining techniques to analyze student data and predict dropout risk. By using classification, clustering, and association rule mining, the project aims to not only predict which students are likely to drop out but also understand the underlying patterns and behaviors that lead to dropout. This helps institutions take timely actions to improve student retention.
